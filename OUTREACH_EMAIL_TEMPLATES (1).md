## Email Best Practices

### Before Sending

- [ ] Research the company (recent funding, job postings, blog posts, incidents)
- [ ] Research the person (their talks, GitHub, LinkedIn, Twitter)
- [ ] Find a specific pain point to reference (don't be generic)
- [ ] Personalize the greeting and one detail in the body
- [ ] Keep it short (3-4 sentences is better than paragraphs)
- [ ] Include ONE clear ask (30-min call, download case study, read essay)

### Subject Lines That Work

✅ **Good:**
- "Configuration drift at [Company]?"
- "Re: [Company]'s [Service] incident"
- "[Mutual contact] recommended I reach out"
- "Deterministic systems at your scale"

❌ **Bad:**
- "Let's talk about systems architecture"
- "Quick question about MSY Protocol"
- "Consulting opportunity"
- "Hi! Check this out"

### Call-to-Action

Every email should have ONE clear next step:

1. **Schedule a call**: "Happy to do a 30-min diagnostic. Are you free Thursday?"
2. **Download asset**: "Here's a case study that might resonate. Let me know what you think."
3. **Read content**: "I wrote an essay on this. Curious for your thoughts."
4. **One-off question**: "Quick question: how do you handle state consistency at 50+ services?"

### Sending Strategy

**Cold outreach target**: 
- 20 emails/week to relevant CTOs/VPs
- Personalize each one (don't template them obviously)
- Track opens, clicks, replies in a spreadsheet
- Follow up after 1 week if no response
- After 2 weeks with no response, move on

**Expected response rate**:
- 5-10% reply rate on cold emails
- 20-30% of replies become calls
- 1-2 calls/week → 1 engagement/month

## Email Sequences for Warm Leads

### Sequence: Prospect Shows Interest

**Email 1** (Day 1): Send case study + 30-min offer
**Email 2** (Day 3): Follow-up if no response: "Did case study resonate?"
**Email 3** (Day 7): Quick link to calendar: "Let me know what works for you"
**Email 4** (Day 14): Alternative: "If MSY isn't the right fit, no hard feelings. But here's an alternative..."

### Sequence: Post-Call

**Email 1** (Day 1): Send agenda/notes from call + next steps
**Email 2** (Day 3): Share relevant case study or essay
**Email 3** (Day 7): "Thoughts on approach? Any questions?"
**Email 4** (Day 14): If stalled: "Where are we in your decision?"

---

## Email Metrics to Track

For each outreach campaign, track:

- **Open rate**: % of emails opened (good target: 25-35%)
- **Click rate**: % who click a link (good target: 5-10%)
- **Reply rate**: % who reply (good target: 5-10%)
- **Call rate**: % of replies that become calls (good target: 50%+)
- **Conversion rate**: % of calls that become engagements (good target: 20-30%)

Adjust subject lines, body copy, and timing based on what works.

## Do's and Don'ts

✅ **DO:**
- Personalize every email (not template obvious)
- Reference specific research about their company
- Show understanding of their problem
- Be direct about what you want (30-min call)
- Keep it short (4-5 sentences max)
- Follow up (2-3 times)
- Include your contact info clearly

❌ **DON'T:**
- Send generic "let's talk" emails
- Lead with your service (lead with their problem)
- Ask for a meeting without context
- Use buzzwords without substance
- Attach long documents in cold email
- Send too many follow-ups (2-3 max)
- Use aggressive language ("Let's grab coffee!")

## Getting Enterprise Conversations Started

1. **Find pain**: Look for recent incidents, job postings, scaling signals
2. **Reference research**: Show you know about them specifically
3. **Offer value first**: Case study, essay, framework—not a sales call
4. **Make ask clear**: "30-min diagnostic. Can you do Thursday?"
5. **Follow up**: After 1 week, then 1 more time
6. **Move on**: If no response after 2 weeks, next prospect

**Expected timeline**: Cold email → 2-3 week sales cycle for first call.
